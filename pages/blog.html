    • Steaming a Canto Fish

    Nov 11, 2025

    When I moved to Seattle, I realized that I had been taking for granted dishes from my culture. Dishes like Cantonese style Steamed Fish are near and dear. After calling my parents and getting their tips, I decided to try my hand at this classic dish.

    When picking a fish to steam, you have two options, live fish and fresh fish at the market. If you can, live white fish is best! Otherwise, here are some tips to help you choose a fresh fish.

    Fresh Fish Signs:

    • clear non cloudy eyes
    • firm flesh
    • scales do not come off
    • gills are still red/pink underneath

    Ingredients

    • fresh white fish
    • 2 sticks green onions(sliced lengthwise)
    • 1 inch ginger julienned
    • 3 tbsp soy sauce
    • 2 tbsp oil

    Supplies

    • steam rack
    • deep pan
    • 10" dish for steaming

    Instructions

    1. Steam Setup: Place the steam rack in a deep pan and fill with water until right below the top of the steam rack. Cover and heat on high.
    2. Fish: Place fish into the dish you'll be using for steaming.
    3. Aromatics: Cut your aromatics. Ginger is sliced, and julienned.
    4. Steam Fish: When the water in the steam pan is boiling, place the dish with the fish onto the steam rack and cover. Steam on high heat for 10-12 minutes depending on the size of the fish.
    5. Stirfry aromatics: While it is steaming, begin heating up 2 tbsp of oil. When hot, quickly toss in aromatics for a few seconds.
    6. Arrange: Once fish is done steaming, take it out, drain the liquid and place stirfried aromatics on top. If the fish finishes steaming sooner, turn off heat and leave covered
    7. Done: Drizzle soy sauce over the fish. Enjoy!
